01. Role purpose

Why this internship exists

This internship exists because Pustakam needs more than visibility. It needs real product movement. That means the product should be seen by the right people, tried by real users, and evaluated through actual user reaction rather than assumptions.

The role is useful because college students are close to live communities, peer conversations, practical learning problems, and the kind of word-of-mouth behavior that helps an early product get traction.

Awareness

Help more people discover that the product exists.

Usage

Get actual people to try Pustakam, not just react to posts.

Learning

Bring back product truth that can improve the platform.

Important framing

This should be treated as a traction-plus-product-learning internship, not just a publicity internship.

02. What Pustakam is

The product they are helping grow

Pustakam is an AI-powered learning-book workflow. A user gives the app a topic or learning goal. The app can refine the topic, create a roadmap, generate chapter-by-chapter content, assemble the final book, and then support reading, editing, analytics, and export.

Product layerWhat happensWhy it matters
InputThe user enters a topic or learning goal.The entry barrier stays low.
PlanningThe app creates a roadmap before writing the full book.The output feels structured rather than random.
GenerationModules are generated one by one with visible progress.The process feels controlled and recoverable.
Post-generationThe book can be read, edited, analyzed, and exported.The output becomes useful beyond a one-time AI result.

Strong product description

Pustakam helps a person turn a topic into a structured learning book.

Weak product description

Pustakam is just an AI writer.

03. Main aim of the internship

What success actually means

The internship is successful when it helps Pustakam move from being seen to being tried, understood, and improved.

1

Find the right people

Not everyone is the right first user. The intern should identify the groups who are most likely to understand and benefit from the product.

2

Get them to try the app

The goal is not just content reach. The goal is actual product usage.

3

Observe real reaction

The intern should notice what people love, where they hesitate, and what they do not understand immediately.

4

Bring back useful truth

The feedback should improve product messaging, onboarding, and product decisions.

04. Core work expected

What the intern will actually do

Product familiarization

Use the app personally, generate books, understand the roadmap flow, and learn how to explain the product simply.

Community discovery

Identify WhatsApp groups, Discord servers, Reddit spaces, student circles, and campus communities where likely users already exist.

Soft outreach

Introduce Pustakam naturally through helpful conversations, demos, and relevant examples rather than spam.

User onboarding

Help a real person try the app with a good first prompt and understand what the product is doing.

Feedback capture

Collect confusion points, objections, reactions, and requests from actual product interactions.

Weekly reporting

Summarize where they shared, who tried the app, what worked, and what should change.

What the role is not

This role is not about random posting, vanity engagement, or sounding polished without producing useful traction.

05. What should be learned from interns

The real value you can extract from them

Interns are not only useful because they can spread the product. They are useful because they can reveal market truth quickly.

Signals to extract

  • Which message makes people understand the product fastest
  • Which audience responds most positively
  • Which use cases feel immediately valuable
  • Which example prompts create the strongest reactions
  • What confusion appears during first use

Product improvements informed by interns

  • Stronger onboarding copy
  • Better first-prompt suggestions
  • Clearer messaging on the landing page
  • Better demo topics for social proof
  • More useful explanations of what the app is and is not
The strongest thing to extract from an early intern is not effort. It is pattern recognition.
06. How interns should talk about Pustakam

Simple explanation and demo guidance

Suggested 60-second explanation

"Pustakam helps someone turn a topic into a full learning book. The app first creates a roadmap, then writes the chapters, then packages everything into something the user can actually read, edit, analyze, and export. It is useful for people who want structured learning content quickly."

Good first demo flow

  • Start with a relatable topic
  • Show prompt entry
  • Show roadmap generation
  • Explain chapter generation
  • Jump to a completed book
  • Show reading mode and export

Good first demo topics

  • Personal finance for beginners
  • Beginner Python
  • Startup basics
  • AI from scratch
  • Productivity systems

Communication rule

Lead with workflow value, not hype. The strongest story is not "the AI is powerful." The strongest story is "the app helps people get a structured learning asset much faster."

07. Weekly reporting and evaluation

What should be tracked each week

The weekly report does not need to be fancy, but it should be clear and useful.

CategoryWhat should be reported
OutreachCommunities explored, posts shared, people contacted, or conversations started
UsageHow many people actually tried the app because of the intern
FeedbackPositive reactions, objections, confusion points, and feature requests
MessagingWhich explanations worked, which did not, and what should be improved
RecommendationsWhat the intern believes should change in product, onboarding, or public positioning

Strong evaluation signals

  • Real users tried the product
  • Good feedback quality came back
  • The intern found strong communities
  • The intern surfaced useful product truth

Weak evaluation signals

  • Only likes or views with no real usage
  • Generic posting with no audience fit
  • Reporting activity without learning
  • Talking about the product without understanding it properly
08. Final perspective

My thoughts on the role

This is a smart use of college interns if the role is handled properly. The strongest value will come not from making them behave like generic marketers, but from using them as early traction and insight collectors.

Why this makes sense

  • They are close to real peer networks
  • They can test messaging quickly
  • They can help reveal what actually makes the product click
  • They can bring useful market reactions early

Best way to use them

  • Give them a clear product explanation
  • Track real-user trials, not only posting effort
  • Ask for product insight every week
  • Use them to improve onboarding and messaging, not just awareness
If handled well, this role gives you three things at once: publicity, user acquisition, and real market learning.
